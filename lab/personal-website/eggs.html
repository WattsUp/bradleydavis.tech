Easter Eggs

You found an Easter egg which is this page, woah! You can reveal the rest of them or continue searching.

  • This page is an Easter egg
  • The home page has a hidden paragraph that can be revealed by hovering over its location
  • My about me portrait is emulated from the Kramer painted in The Letter episode, Seinfeld
  • When the Brad in the footer is clicked, he runs away. If you click him many times quickly, he freaks out. #featurenotabug
  • Interactive resume eggs
    • The about me particle display is emulated from the Wakanda technology vibranium sand from Black Panther
    • The WSU blinky display is a workable LCD circuit using an STM microcontroller
    • My rocket's engines are the F1 engine as found on the powerful Saturn V
    • The boxes in the experience rooms are numbered:
      • Room 1: 4, 8, 15, 16, 23, 42. A nod to Lost's The Numbers
      • Room 2: L-42, I-42, F-42, E-42. The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy's The Answer to the Ultimate Question of Life, the Universe, and Everything is 42
      • Room 3: P-22 on top of I-7, 22/7 is an approximation of pi (π)
      • Room 3: 19 on top of 7, 19/7 is an approximation of e
    • The rocket bridge
      • Many of the information displays are emulated from the original series Star Trek, including the random blinky lights on the console, and The Next Generation's LCARS
      • There is a Lorentz transformation on one of the displays
      • The trajectory is a Hohmann transfer with the planets and mars in the proper location
    • The Mallard Madness duck has been assimilated by the Borg, resistance is futile
    • The shuttlecraft is taken from Star Trek
    • Some of the instruments on the Mars lander can be found on the Insight Lander
  • On the culinary pages, my name turns into Breadley
  • The home page has an ASCII art tesla coil in the HTML comments
