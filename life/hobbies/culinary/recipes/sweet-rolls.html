Sweet Rolls

I prefer raspberry, others prefer the classic cinnamon

Ingredients

Makes nine large rolls

  • 2.25 tsp instant dry yeast (1 package)
  • 1 cups warm milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 0.25 cup sugar
  • 0.5 tbsp salt
  • 3-4 cups flour, bread preferred, all-purpose okay
    • Amount varies by environmental factors
  • 1 tbsp soft butter

Raspberry Filling

  • 1 cup raspberries, fresh or frozen, not juiced
  • 0.5 cup white sugar

Cinnamon Filling

  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp cinnamon

Directions

You can use a stand mixer with a dough hook to mix and knead the dough

  1. Mix the yeast, milk, egg, and sugar in a bowl, let sit 5 minutes
  2. Add the salt and some flour
  3. Gradually add flour and mix until it looks like bread dough
    • Not wet, not dry
    • Shaggy
  4. Move it to a floured surface and knead it for a while.
    • Soft
    • Pliable
  5. Lightly grease a large bowl, place the kneaded dough inside. Cover and let rise until doubled, about an hour. I create a proofing oven by placing a pot of boiling water in my oven beside the dough bowl. As the water evaporates, it warms and humidifies the air.
  6. After the first rise, punch down the dough and gently knead a handful of time more
  7. Roll flat into a rectangle 9" wide and 1/3" thick
  8. Spread butter on top
  9. Sprinkle desired filling evenly over buttered dough
  10. Roll up into a log similar to a swiss roll
  11. Slice log into nine pieces, 1" thick
  12. Grease a 9"x9" pan and place the slices inside. Cover and let rise 30 more minutes. I use the same proofing oven technique
  13. Preheat oven to 375Â° F
  14. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown. If it smells burnt, it has been too long.
  15. Cool slightly, spread frosting on, and serve warm

Cream Cheese Frosting

Ingredients

  • 8 oz soft cream cheese (1 package)
  • 7 tbsp soft butter (or 1 stick, I just use the remainder from the rolls)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 cups powered sugar
  • 0.5 cup fresh raspberries (optional, I just really like raspberries)

Directions

I recommend a stand mixer with spatula paddle.

  • Combine cream cheese and butter together until smooth
  • Add powered sugar slowly while mixing
  • Add remaining ingredients
  • Beat until smooth, beat for longer for lighter frosting (adds air)
